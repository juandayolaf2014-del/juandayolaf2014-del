About Me

I'm a graduate student at Cornell University pursuing a Master of Professional Studies in Applied Economics and Management, concentrating in Behavioral Marketing and Behavioral Finance. My passion lies in understanding how psychology shapes economic decisions — and applying that insight to create value in finance and marketing.

My academic journey spans four universities across four countries — Cornell (USA), Aalto University (Finland), Bocconi (Italy), and Toulouse Business School (France) — giving me a genuinely international perspective on global business.

Professionally, I've contributed to M&A strategy at Empresas Polar across Latin America, and to applied research at the Cornell Emerging Markets Institute, co-authoring a working paper on billion-dollar companies in developing economies.

I'm actively seeking roles at the intersection of behavioral economics, finance, and marketing where I can translate theory into strategic impact.
